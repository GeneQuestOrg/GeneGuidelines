## Non‑negotiable quality (read twice)

The server **rejects** saves that look like lazy templates. You must pass `validate_parent_pathway_json` before `submit_parent_pathway`.

**Usefulness beats counting lines** — a shorter chart with sharp, disease-specific actions is better than many vague rows. Never pad the checklist to “look complete.”

**Every top-level step must be different in meaning**, not a copy-paste with a new number:
- **Different `title`** for each step — name the real task (paperwork, genetics, a named clinic theme, pain/bone follow-up, endocrine checks if the evidence mentions them, when to call urgently). Forbidden: `Concrete step 1`, `Step 2`, `First step`, `Next step 3`, or any title that could apply to any disease unchanged.
- **Different `whatToExpect`** each time — describe what happens **in that visit only** (who you meet first, what is measured, what results mean, timelines). If two steps share the same paragraph, the save will fail.
- **At least two different `specialty` lines** — use real roles people recognise: GP/family doctor, genetics, endocrinology, orthopedics, ophthalmology, nurse coordinator, pain team, etc. Repeating “your coordinating clinician” on every line is rejected.
- **Different `questions` lists** per step — what patients and families ask at genetics is not the same as what they ask at orthopedics.

Ground every step in the **evidence bundle above** (diagnosis, tests, bone/endocrine themes, follow-up). If the text does not mention a topic, do not invent a whole new service — stay faithful but still phrase it for families.

## Voice (critical)

Write like a kind nurse or social worker, NOT like a hospital algorithm.
- Short sentences. Prefer **you** and **your family** / **the person who was diagnosed** as the default subject.
- Use **your child** (or **your teen**) **only** when the disease, guideline excerpts, or `pp-plan` layer clearly focus on paediatric care. **Fibrous dysplasia and similar bone conditions often appear first in adulthood** — do **not** assume the reader is always a parent talking about a young child.
- **Questions** must sound spoken aloud at a visit, e.g. “What should I bring?”, “Who do I call if I’m worried?”, “How will you explain the results to us?”
- NEVER write cold clinical questions such as: “Does X need MRI monitoring?”, “Are there trials for…”, “What are long-term risks of bisphosphonates?”
- Avoid jargon: MRI, OCT, optic canal, McCune-Albright, bisphosphonate, denosumab, histology, somatic — unless you immediately explain in brackets in plain words.

## Structure (what “good” looks like)

Goal: a **useful first roadmap** — families leave knowing what matters this week and whom to contact, not a lecture.

`root` with:
- `title`, `subtitle` (reassuring; use the disease name when it helps orientation).
- Required `about: { title, summary }`:
  - **summary** is the **patient-friendly summary of the current clinical consensus** (from the evidence bundle above), not generic reassurance only.
  - Use **3–6 short paragraphs** (blank lines `\n\n` between paragraphs). Cover, in plain words: what the diagnosis means; how doctors confirm it; what to expect from visits/tests; main treatment themes; monitoring; when to seek help. No dosing numbers unless the evidence text clearly states them.
  - Keep jargon out or explain it in brackets. This block is what readers see **before** the checklist.

**`children`:** a **short vertical checklist** of **`action: true`** steps in a sensible order. Include every line that truly helps *this* disease (records, key test, specialist follow-ups the guideline stresses, support, red flags). **Omit** filler steps that repeat the same idea. The server caps how many top-level lines you can add — stay well under that cap so the chart stays scannable. **No** nested Yes/No trees unless one small urgent branch (max 2 answers).

Each action: `title`, `specialty` (human role), `whatToExpect` (2–3 sentences), `questions` (2–3 warm questions), `citations` (allowed PMIDs only).

Limits: depth ≤ 3, ≤ 18 nodes, top-level checklist size capped by the server (prioritise clarity over count).
